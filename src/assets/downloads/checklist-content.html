Pre-Construction

  • Construction schedule confirmed
  • Phasing plan confirmed (building, floor, or unit sequencing)
  • Primary communication path and approval authority identified
  • Product data sheets approved for all countertop materials
  • Physical samples approved
  • Millwork / casework shop drawings approved
  • Plumbing fixtures approved (sinks, faucets, accessories)
  • Sink responsibility confirmed (countertop contractor vs others)
  • Countertop preliminary drawings approved (plumbing/cutouts, overhangs, seams, joints, backsplash)
  • Non-standard details or tolerances documented
  • Site constraints identified for material staging, unloading, and installation

Pre-Production

  • All sinks provided by others delivered onsite
  • Sinks distributed into each unit prior to field measure
  • Field measure scheduled (minimum 7 days lead time)
  • Field measure scheduled 1+ business days after casework completion
  • Casework installation 100% complete, clean, and accessible
  • No ongoing trade work that would prevent field measure completion
  • Install scheduled 7–10 days after field measure
  • Final install date confirmed (field measure complete, material available, site ready)
Tip: Field measurements should be scheduled immediately after casework installation to ensure optimal fit and minimize delays.

Material Selection

  • Review material options (Granite, Quartz, Marble, Porcelain)
  • Consider durability requirements for high-traffic areas
  • Evaluate maintenance requirements
  • Compare cost vs. performance for your budget
  • Select edge profiles (straight, beveled, bullnose, ogee)
  • Choose finish type (polished, honed, leathered)
  • Confirm material availability and lead times
  • Request material samples for approval

Timeline Planning

  • Casework & Finish Carpentry Material Deliveries (Typical Production: One 53ft. Flatbed Delivery per Day or Two 26ft Box truck Deliveries per Day)
  • Casework Installation (Typical Production: 2-4 Apartment Units per Day)
  • Countertop Field Measurements (Typical Production: 10-20 Apartment Units per Day)
  • Countertop Fabrication (Typical Lead Time: 5-7 Days)
  • Finish Trim & Door Installation (Typical Production: 2-4 Apartment Units per Day)
  • Countertop Installation (Typical Production: 5-20 Apartment Units per Day)
  • Miscellaneous Finish Carpentry Items (Handles & Accessories) (Typical Production: 3-8 Apartment Units per Day)
Note: Actual timelines vary based on project size, material availability, and coordination with other trades. Always build in buffer time for unexpected delays.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not scheduling measurements early enough
  • Choosing materials without considering maintenance
  • Underestimating timeline requirements
  • Not coordinating with other trades
  • Not having a clear change order process
  • Not providing a clear access point to load materials into building
