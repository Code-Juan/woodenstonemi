Pre-Installation Requirements

  • Confirm project timeline and unit count
    • Small projects (10-20 units): 1-2 weeks
    • Medium projects (50-100 units): 2-4 weeks
    • Large projects (100+ units): 4-8 weeks
  • Verify casework/cabinetry installation is complete
  • Ensure electrical and plumbing rough-ins are finished
  • Confirm wall finishes are complete (paint, tile, etc.)
  • Schedule field measurements after casework installation
  • Coordinate access to all units with property management
  • Verify delivery access and staging area availability
  • Confirm elevator or loading dock access if applicable
Tip: Field measurements should be scheduled immediately after casework installation to ensure optimal fit and minimize delays.

Material Selection

  • Review material options (Granite, Quartz, Marble, Porcelain)
  • Consider durability requirements for high-traffic areas
  • Evaluate maintenance requirements
  • Compare cost vs. performance for your budget
  • Select edge profiles (straight, beveled, bullnose, ogee)
  • Choose finish type (polished, honed, leathered)
  • Confirm material availability and lead times
  • Request material samples for approval

Timeline Planning

  • Week 1: Casework installation and field measurements
  • Week 2-3: Countertop fabrication (varies by project size)
  • Week 3-4: Finish trim carpentry installation
  • Week 4-5: Countertop installation
  • Final: Quality inspection and walkthrough
Note: Actual timelines vary based on project size, material availability, and coordination with other trades. Always build in buffer time for unexpected delays.

Budget Planning

  • Material costs (typically $50-120/sq ft depending on material)
  • Fabrication costs
  • Installation labor
  • Sink fixtures and cutouts
  • Edge profiles and finishes
  • Delivery and staging
  • Permits and inspections (if required)
  • Waste disposal
  • Contingency (10-15% recommended)

Questions to Ask Your Contractor

  • What is your experience with projects of this size?
  • Can you provide references from similar projects?
  • What is your typical timeline for [X] units?
  • How do you handle coordination with other trades?
  • What warranty do you provide on workmanship?
  • Do you handle permits and inspections?
  • What is your process for quality control?
  • How do you handle change orders?
  • What payment schedule do you require?
  • Are you licensed and insured?

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not scheduling measurements early enough
  • Choosing materials without considering maintenance
  • Underestimating timeline requirements
  • Not coordinating with other trades
  • Selecting contractor based solely on lowest price
  • Not reviewing contractor's portfolio and references
  • Failing to account for waste and breakage in budget
  • Not having a clear change order process
