Granite

Cost Range: $50-80/sq ft

✓ Pros

  • Extremely durable and heat-resistant
  • Natural stone, unique patterns
  • Good value for durability
  • Resistant to scratches
  • Long-lasting (decades)

✗ Cons

  • Requires annual sealing
  • Can chip at edges
  • Heavy (requires strong cabinets)
  • Limited color consistency

Best Use Cases: High-traffic kitchens, commercial kitchens, areas requiring heat resistance

Maintenance: Clean with mild soap and water. Seal annually with penetrating sealer.

Quartz

Cost Range: $60-90/sq ft

✓ Pros

  • No sealing required
  • Non-porous (hygienic)
  • Consistent color and pattern
  • Stain-resistant
  • Low maintenance
  • Wide variety of colors

✗ Cons

  • Not as heat-resistant as granite
  • Can be damaged by excessive heat
  • Seams may be visible
  • Higher cost than granite

Best Use Cases: Bathrooms, kitchens, healthcare facilities, food service areas

Maintenance: Clean with mild soap and water. No sealing needed.

Marble

Cost Range: $80-120/sq ft

✓ Pros

  • Elegant, luxurious appearance
  • Natural stone beauty
  • Cool surface (good for baking)
  • Unique veining patterns

✗ Cons

  • Stains easily (acid-sensitive)
  • Requires frequent sealing
  • Can scratch and etch
  • Higher maintenance
  • Not ideal for high-traffic

Best Use Cases: Low-traffic areas, luxury projects, bathrooms, feature walls

Maintenance: Clean immediately after spills. Seal every 6-12 months. Use cutting boards and coasters.

Porcelain

Cost Range: $70-100/sq ft

✓ Pros

  • Extremely durable
  • Stain and scratch resistant
  • UV resistant (outdoor use)
  • Low maintenance
  • Can mimic natural stone
  • Non-porous

✗ Cons

  • Can chip if hit hard
  • Limited edge profile options
  • Higher cost
  • Requires skilled installation

Best Use Cases: Outdoor areas, high-abuse commercial spaces, modern designs

Maintenance: Clean with mild soap and water. Very low maintenance.

Quartzite

Cost Range: $75-110/sq ft

✓ Pros

  • Harder than granite
  • Heat and scratch resistant
  • Natural stone beauty
  • Unique patterns
  • Durable for commercial use

✗ Cons

  • Requires sealing
  • Can be expensive
  • Heavy material
  • Limited availability

Best Use Cases: Luxury kitchens, high-end projects, areas needing maximum durability

Maintenance: Clean with mild soap. Seal annually.

ROI Considerations

  • Initial Cost vs. Longevity: Higher upfront costs often mean lower maintenance and longer lifespan
  • Maintenance Costs: Factor in sealing, cleaning products, and repair costs over time
  • Property Value: Quality countertops can increase property value and appeal
  • Tenant Satisfaction: Durable, attractive countertops reduce complaints and turnover
  • Warranty: Consider manufacturer and installer warranties
